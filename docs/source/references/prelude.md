Inko automatically imports certain symbols into your modules. These symbols are
part of what is called "the prelude".

The prelude includes the following types and methods:

|=
| Symbol
| Source module
|-
| `Array`
| [](std.array)
|-
| `Boolean`
| [](std.bool)
|-
| `ByteArray`
| [](std.bytes)
|-
| `Float`
| [](std.float)
|-
| `Int`
| [](std.int)
|-
| `Map`
| [](std.map)
|-
| `Nil`
| [](std.nil)
|-
| `Option`
| [](std.option)
|-
| `Result`
| [](std.result)
|-
| `String`
| [](std.string)
|-
| `panic`
| [](std.process)
